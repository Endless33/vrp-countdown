# Future Access Path

This document describes the potential access path for organizations seeking VRP evaluation after the conclusion of the public pilot window.

Current public pilot intake deadline:

**August 31, 2026 — 00:00 UTC**

---

## Important

The public pilot intake window is time-limited.

After the deadline, public intake may no longer be available.

Organizations should not assume that future access will remain available under the same conditions.

---

## No Automatic Continuation

The existence of a public pilot program does not imply permanent access.

The conclusion of the public pilot window does not automatically create:

- A second pilot window
- A waiting list
- A reservation queue
- Automatic future participation

No such mechanisms are guaranteed.

---

## Future Requests

After the deadline, organizations seeking access may be required to initiate contact directly.

Such requests may require:

- Organizational information
- Operational environment description
- Continuity requirements
- Technical objectives
- Evaluation rationale

Future requests may be reviewed individually.

---

## Review Process

Future reviews may consider:

- Technical relevance
- Infrastructure complexity
- Continuity requirements
- Evaluation quality
- Validation value
- Available capacity

Review criteria may evolve over time.

---

## No Guaranteed Response

Submission of a request does not guarantee:

- Review
- Response
- Acceptance
- Evaluation
- Participation

A request may receive no response.

A request may be declined.

A request may be deferred.

---

## Availability

Future availability may become:

- Limited
- Restricted
- Invitation-only
- Capacity-constrained
- Temporarily unavailable

The protocol is not dependent on maintaining an open access model.

---

## Why This Exists

The objective of the public pilot program is validation.

The objective is not unlimited distribution.

As validation progresses, participation requirements may change.

The access model may change.

The review process may change.

The availability model may change.

---

## Public Evidence

Public evidence remains available.

Validation reports remain available.

Observable behavior remains available.

Organizations may continue reviewing public validation artifacts regardless of pilot participation status.

---

## Protected Core

Protected Core remains protected.

Future access requests should not be interpreted as requests for unrestricted source access.

The Protected Core boundary remains unchanged.

---

## Long-Term Position

VRP development continues.

Validation continues.

Testing continues.

Evidence continues.

What changes over time is access.

Not the existence of the protocol.

---

## Final Statement

The public pilot window is an opportunity.

It is not a permanent entitlement.

Future access may exist.

Future access may not exist.

Organizations should make decisions based on currently available opportunities rather than assumptions about future availability.
