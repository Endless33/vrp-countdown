# VRP Pilot Window Closed

The public VRP pilot intake window is time-limited.

Current public pilot intake closes on:

**August 31, 2026 — 00:00 UTC**

After this deadline, the public pilot intake may be closed.

This means that new public pilot applications may no longer be accepted through the standard intake flow.

---

## What This Means

The end of the public pilot window does not mean that VRP development stops.

It does not mean that validation stops.

It does not mean that testing stops.

It means that access changes.

After the deadline, participation may become:

- Restricted
- Invitation-only
- Individually reviewed
- Limited to one organization
- Unavailable to new applicants

No future public pilot window is guaranteed.

---

## Public Intake Status

Before the deadline:

- GitHub pilot applications may be submitted
- Private evaluation requests may be submitted
- Organizations may request pilot consideration
- Evaluation discussions may begin

After the deadline:

- Public GitHub pilot intake may be closed
- Private request forms may be closed or restricted
- New requests may require direct email review
- Review may be discretionary
- Response is not guaranteed
- Acceptance is not guaranteed

---

## Protected Core Boundary

VRP public evidence remains public.

VRP Protected Core remains protected.

Public validation artifacts may show:

- Runtime behavior
- Evidence logs
- Validation summaries
- Observable outcomes
- Rejection verdicts
- Recovery results

They do not expose protected internal protocol logic.

That boundary is intentional.

---

## Why The Window Closes

The public pilot window exists to create a controlled evaluation period.

It is not intended to remain open indefinitely.

The goal is validation quality, not participant quantity.

VRP is not being distributed as unrestricted software access.

The pilot window exists for organizations that have real continuity-sensitive workloads and meaningful failure scenarios to evaluate.

---

## After Closure

After the public pilot window closes, future access decisions may require direct author review.

Such review may consider:

- Technical relevance
- Operational environment
- Continuity requirements
- Evaluation seriousness
- Validation value
- Available capacity

Submission after the deadline does not guarantee review, response, acceptance, or access.

---

## Continuity

Development continues.

Validation continues.

Testing continues.

Evidence continues.

The protocol continues.

The public pilot window may close.

VRP does not.
