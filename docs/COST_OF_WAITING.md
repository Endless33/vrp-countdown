Cost Of Waiting

This document is not about VRP.

It is about delay.

A common assumption in technology evaluation is:

"We can always look at it later."

Sometimes that assumption is correct.

Sometimes it is expensive.

---

What Changes While Waiting

Networks continue to evolve.

Infrastructure continues to grow.

Dependencies continue to accumulate.

Operational complexity continues to increase.

Failure modes do not pause while evaluations are delayed.

---

Unknowns Do Not Disappear

A continuity problem does not disappear because it is not measured.

A recovery problem does not disappear because it is not tested.

An authority problem does not disappear because it is not observed.

The absence of validation is not evidence of correctness.

---

Deferred Evaluation

Every organization eventually evaluates risk.

The only variable is timing.

Some evaluate before failure.

Some evaluate after failure.

Both approaches produce information.

The cost is usually different.

---

Public Evidence

VRP public evidence exists so that evaluation can begin before participation.

Logs are public.

Reports are public.

Validation artifacts are public.

The question is not whether evidence exists.

The question is whether it is reviewed.

---

Pilot Window

The public pilot window is temporary.

The protocol is not.

The deadline exists because availability changes.

Not because development stops.

---

Future Access

Future access may exist.

Future access may not exist.

Future review may be available.

Future review may not be available.

Assumptions about future availability should not be confused with guarantees.

---

Final Question

The question is not:

"Will VRP still exist later?"

The answer is yes.

The question is:

"What information would have been available if evaluation had started earlier?"
