# Protected Core Boundary

This document describes the separation between public validation artifacts and protected implementation components.

The boundary is intentional.

The boundary is permanent.

The boundary exists regardless of pilot participation.

---

## Core Principle

VRP follows a simple rule:

Observable behavior may be public.

Protected implementation remains protected.

The objective is to allow external evaluation without requiring disclosure of proprietary protocol logic.

---

## Public Side

The public side may include:

- Validation reports
- Runtime evidence
- Recovery demonstrations
- Replay rejection demonstrations
- Authority transition demonstrations
- Blackout recovery demonstrations
- Continuity validation artifacts
- Public documentation
- Public repositories

The purpose of the public side is verification.

---

## Protected Side

The protected side may include:

- Internal protocol logic
- Protected runtime mechanisms
- Proprietary validation models
- Private implementation details
- Internal decision processes
- Commercial runtime components
- Future protected capabilities

The purpose of the protected side is preservation.

---

## Why The Boundary Exists

The objective of public validation is not source distribution.

The objective is not unrestricted technology transfer.

The objective is not implementation disclosure.

The objective is evidence.

VRP was designed to be evaluated through behavior.

Not through unrestricted access to implementation details.

---

## Observable Without Disclosure

A replay rejection can be observed.

A recovery event can be observed.

A continuity outcome can be observed.

An authority transition can be observed.

The existence of an outcome does not require disclosure of every internal mechanism that produced it.

---

## Evidence Model

Public evidence focuses on:

- What happened
- When it happened
- What was accepted
- What was rejected
- What was preserved
- What failed
- What recovered

Evidence exists to support verification.

Not replication.

---

## Pilot Access

Pilot participation does not automatically remove the Protected Core boundary.

Evaluation access should not be interpreted as unrestricted access.

Protected implementation components may remain protected regardless of evaluation status.

---

## Public Repositories

Public repositories are intended to demonstrate:

- Concepts
- Validation approaches
- Observable behavior
- Evidence generation

They are not intended to disclose the entirety of VRP.

A public repository is evidence.

It is not the complete system.

---

## Common Misunderstanding

A common assumption is:

"If enough reports, logs, and demonstrations are published, the entire system becomes visible."

This assumption is incorrect.

The public layer is intentionally designed to expose outcomes.

Not complete implementation.

---

## Long-Term Position

As VRP evolves:

Public Evidence remains public.

Validation remains public.

Observable behavior remains public.

Protected Core remains protected.

The boundary does not disappear because interest increases.

The boundary does not disappear because validation succeeds.

The boundary exists by design.

---

## Final Statement

VRP is intended to be observable.

VRP is not intended to be fully disclosed.

Public Evidence remains public.

Protected Core remains protected.
