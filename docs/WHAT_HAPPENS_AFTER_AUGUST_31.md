# What Happens After August 31

A common assumption is that a deadline is primarily a marketing event.

This document exists to clarify that assumption.

The August 31 deadline is not intended as a marketing event.

It is an operational boundary.

---

## What Does Not Change

After August 31:

- VRP development continues
- Validation continues
- Testing continues
- Evidence generation continues
- Public reports remain available
- Public repositories remain available

The protocol continues to evolve.

The protocol does not depend on pilot participation.

---

## What Changes

The public pilot intake may close.

The public application process may close.

The public evaluation pathway may close.

The public opportunity may end.

The protocol continues.

Access changes.

---

## Pilot Status

Prior to August 31:

- Organizations may apply through the public intake process
- Organizations may request evaluation
- Organizations may begin pilot discussions

After August 31:

- Public intake may be closed
- New applications may no longer be accepted
- Future participation may become selective
- Future participation may become invitation-only
- Future participation may require direct review

No future intake round is guaranteed.

---

## Access Assumptions

Organizations should not assume:

- A future public intake window
- A future application period
- A future pilot opportunity
- A future evaluation pathway

Future availability is not guaranteed.

---

## Why The Deadline Exists

The pilot window exists because validation is valuable.

The pilot window does not exist because validation is required.

VRP development is not dependent on pilot participation.

The protocol continues either way.

The deadline exists to define a bounded evaluation period.

Nothing more.

Nothing less.

---

## Public Evidence

Public evidence remains available.

Logs remain available.

Reports remain available.

Validation artifacts remain available.

Organizations remain free to evaluate public evidence independently.

---

## Protected Core

Protected Core remains protected.

The conclusion of the pilot window does not alter the Protected Core boundary.

The conclusion of the pilot window does not create unrestricted access.

The conclusion of the pilot window does not imply future disclosure.

---

## Future Communication

Future updates may become less frequent.

Not because development stopped.

Because development and validation require time.

Some technologies spend long periods in public discussion.

Others spend long periods in quiet execution.

VRP is gradually moving toward the second category.

---

## Final Statement

August 31 is not a countdown to the end of VRP.

It is a countdown to the end of the current public pilot intake.

The protocol continues.

The work continues.

The evidence continues.

The deadline remains real.
