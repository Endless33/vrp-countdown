# Post-Deadline Access Policy

This document describes the intended access model after the conclusion of the current public VRP pilot window.

Current public pilot intake deadline:

**August 31, 2026 — 00:00 UTC**

---

## Purpose

The public pilot window was created to allow a limited number of organizations to evaluate VRP during an active validation period.

The pilot window is not intended to remain permanently available.

The purpose of the deadline is to establish a bounded evaluation period and a clear transition point.

---

## What Changes

After the public pilot window closes, the protocol remains under active development.

Validation remains active.

Testing remains active.

Evidence generation remains active.

What changes is access.

---

## Public Applications

After the deadline:

- Public pilot applications may no longer be accepted
- Public intake forms may be retired
- Public intake channels may be disabled
- New applicants may no longer have access to a standard submission process

The existence of a previous pilot program should not be interpreted as a guarantee of future participation opportunities.

---

## Future Requests

Organizations seeking access after the deadline may be required to initiate contact directly.

Such contact may require:

- Organizational identification
- Technical evaluation goals
- Operational context
- Continuity requirements
- Intended validation scope

Submission of a request does not guarantee:

- Review
- Response
- Evaluation
- Participation
- Access

---

## Selection Philosophy

Future participation decisions may consider:

- Technical relevance
- Continuity requirements
- Infrastructure complexity
- Evaluation quality
- Validation value
- Available review capacity

VRP is evaluated through selective participation rather than unrestricted access.

---

## Availability

Future participation may be:

- Limited
- Selective
- Invitation-only
- Individually reviewed
- Temporarily unavailable

The protocol does not depend on maintaining a public intake process.

---

## Commercial Model

Future evaluation structures, licensing models, pilot programs, integration opportunities, and participation requirements may differ from those available during the public pilot window.

Organizations should not assume that future access conditions will match historical access conditions.

---

## Public Evidence

Public evidence remains public.

Validation reports remain public.

Observable runtime behavior remains public.

Protected Core remains protected.

The purpose of public evidence is verification.

The purpose of the Protected Core boundary is preservation of proprietary implementation.

---

## Final Statement

The closure of the public pilot window does not represent the end of VRP.

It represents the end of a specific access period.

Development continues.

Validation continues.

Testing continues.

The protocol continues.
