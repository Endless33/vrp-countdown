# Pilot Selection Model

This document describes the principles used to evaluate participation requests for VRP-related pilot activities.

The purpose of the pilot program is not scale.

The purpose is validation.

VRP is evaluated through selective participation and controlled environments.

---

## Primary Objective

The objective of a pilot is to generate meaningful validation evidence.

The objective is not maximizing participant count.

The objective is not maximizing exposure.

The objective is not maximizing repository traffic.

Validation quality is prioritized over pilot quantity.

---

## What Makes A Strong Candidate

Strong candidates typically have one or more of the following characteristics:

- Continuity-sensitive workloads
- Complex network environments
- Multi-region infrastructure
- Mobile or unstable transport conditions
- Recovery-sensitive execution paths
- Existing reconnect-related operational pain

Examples include:

- Telecommunications
- Edge infrastructure
- Industrial systems
- Distributed platforms
- Robotics
- Mobile systems
- High-availability environments

---

## What Is Evaluated

Participation requests may be evaluated based on:

### Technical Relevance

Does the environment align with the problems VRP attempts to address?

### Evaluation Quality

Is the organization attempting to validate specific failure conditions?

### Operational Value

Would a successful evaluation produce meaningful evidence?

### Continuity Requirements

Does continuity matter operationally?

### Infrastructure Complexity

Does the environment expose realistic failure scenarios?

---

## Strong Applications

Examples of strong evaluation goals:

- Session continuity during transport migration
- Authority preservation during failover
- Recovery after regional isolation
- Replay containment verification
- Runtime continuity under network instability
- Blackout recovery validation

Specific problems produce stronger evaluations than generic interest.

---

## Weak Applications

Examples of low-information requests:

- "Interested in innovation."
- "Want to learn more."
- "Looking at future technologies."
- "General curiosity."

Such requests provide limited validation value.

---

## Capacity

Pilot capacity is intentionally limited.

Participation decisions may be influenced by:

- Active evaluation load
- Validation priorities
- Available review capacity
- Existing pilot commitments

Not all applicants will be accepted.

---

## Access Philosophy

VRP is not distributed through unrestricted access.

Participation is intended to be deliberate, focused, and technically meaningful.

The objective is to generate evidence.

Not noise.

---

## Public Evidence

Public evidence remains available to everyone.

Validation reports remain public.

Observable behavior remains public.

Protected Core remains protected.

Pilot participation is not required to review public evidence.

Pilot participation exists for organizations that wish to perform deeper evaluation.

---

## Final Principle

The strongest pilot applications are usually the simplest.

A clear problem.

A clear environment.

A clear evaluation goal.

A clear reason why continuity matters.
